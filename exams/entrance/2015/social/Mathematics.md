### Question 1

A student took 5 Model Exams and scored **85**, **88**, **90** and **92** in four subjects (English, Geography, Economics and History). She did not know her score in Mathematics, but she knew that her **mean score in all five exams is 90**. What is her score in Mathematics?

- **A.** 95  
- **B.** 89  
- **C.** 90  
- **D.** 93  

**Solution.**  
Sum of all five scores \(= 5 \times 90 = 450\).  
Known sum \(= 85 + 88 + 90 + 92 = 355\).  
Mathematics score \(= 450 - 355 = 95\).  

**Answer: A**

### Question 4

Which one of the following is true about the function \(f\) defined by \(f(x)=\sqrt{5x-7}\) (reading from booklet)?

- **A.** Domain of \(f\) is \(\{x\in\mathbb{R}\mid x\ge 7\}\).  
- **B.** Range of \(f\) is \(\{f(x)\in\mathbb{R}\mid f(x)\ge 1\}\).  
- **C.** Range of \(f\) is \(\{f(x)\in\mathbb{R}\mid f(x)>0\}\).  
- **D.** Domain of \(f\) is \(\left\{x\in\mathbb{R}\mid x\ge \dfrac{7}{5}\right\}\).  

**Solution.**  
For \(f(x)=\sqrt{5x-7}\), need \(5x-7\ge 0 \Rightarrow x\ge \dfrac{7}{5}\).  
So **D** matches the correct domain (if D states \(x\ge 7/5\)).

**Answer: D** *(if D is \(x\ge 7/5\))*

### Question 8

Which statement is true?

- **A.** For every real number there is exactly one point on the number line.  
- **B.** No irrational corresponds to a point on the number line.  
- **C.** A point for a rational can also represent an irrational (same point).  
- **D.** The integers are in one-to-one correspondence with **all** points on the line.  

**Solution.**  
**A** is the standard completeness/consistency statement for the real line. **D** is false (reals are uncountable). **B** and **C** are false as stated.  

**Answer: A**

### Question 9

Which one **defines a prime number**?

- **A.** A natural number with 3 as a factor.  
- **B.** A natural number with **exactly two distinct** positive divisors.  
- **C.** An odd natural with only two factors.  
- **D.** A natural number with an odd number of factors.  

**Solution.**  
By definition, a prime has exactly two distinct positive divisors (1 and itself). **B** is correct (**C** incorrectly restricts to odd primes).  

**Answer: B**

### Question 10

Which one is a **vector quantity**?

- **A.** Temperature of a room  
- **B.** Speed of a motorbike  
- **C.** Floor area of a classroom  
- **D.** Force to push a car  

**Solution.**  
Force has direction and magnitude → vector. Speed and temperature and area are scalars (or use “velocity” as vector; “speed” is scalar).  

**Answer: D**
